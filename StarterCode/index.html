Analysis
  1. Lacks Healthcare vs In Poverty: There is a positive correlation. The highest rates of poverty tend to imply less healthcare coverage.
  2. Lacks Healthcare vs Household Income: There is a negative correlation, the higher Household Income tend to imply more healthcare coverage.
  3. Lacks Healthcare vs Age(median): There is no evidence of correlation.
  4. Smokes (%) vs In Poverty: There is a positive correlation. When more smokers in the State you may find more poor people living there.
  5. Smokes (%) vs Household Income: There is a negative correlation, the lower Household Income tend to imply more smokers.

The current analysis is just based on the data plotted in the graphs. We recommend more statistical processes to evaluate these results.
